Imagining an Inclusive Future

Connecting grassroots leaders, researchers, and funders to scale disability inclusion across the Global South and the world.

Explore the Fellowship Learn about IFDI

After 13 years of experience and learning with the India Inclusion Foundation (IIF), we are taking the next step to build a globally connected, equitable, and innovation-driven ecosystem that supports disability inclusion—particularly in under-resourced and marginalized regions across the Global South and Africa.

Our Vision

A future where disability inclusion is embedded into how we design technology, fund solutions, and shape public policy.

We advocate for the active participation of persons with disabilities in the development of technological and policy innovations, ensuring inclusion is built into design, decision-making, and implementation—especially in the Global South and Africa.

Disability inclusion is recognized not only as a social justice goal, but as an integral part of development, governance, health, education, urban planning, and artificial intelligence ethics.

The divide between small grassroots groups and large philanthropic institutions is narrowed through equitable funding partnerships and intermediary support.

Cross-cultural and interdisciplinary learning informs the development of inclusive public policies, educational systems, products, and services.

Our Values

Principles that guide how we work, whom we center, and what we build.

Equity Over Charity

We listen to learn. Building trust with each other and those we serve is core to our mission.

Representation Matters

Those closest to the issue must lead the solutions. We prioritize community-led approaches and lived experience in decision-making.

Learning Across Borders

We are systems-thinkers. We aim to contribute meaningful and sustainable solutions.

Every Effort Counts

From solo advocates to large coalitions, every contribution is part of the collective change we seek to build.

Fellowship

Empowering inclusive storytelling to influence policy and perception.

In a country where millions of persons with disabilities remain unseen and unheard, this fellowship seeks to amplify their voices through powerful, rights-based storytelling that can influence hearts, minds, and policy.
